5 ! SETS UP A LIST OF THE TWENTY SIX NEIGHBOURING CELLS OF EACH OF
6 ! THE SMALL CELLS IN THE CENTRAL BOX.
7 
8 ! There are 6 cells for each face of the cell, 12 cells for each edge and 8 cells for each vertex. 26 neighbour cells for each cell
9 
10 ! In the vector MAP is stored the position of the 13 neighbours cells for each cell, the counter (IMAP) advances in 13 of 13.
11 ! e.g. For the cell 1
12 ! ICELL(1,1,1) = 1
13 ! IMAP = (ICELL ( IX, IY, IZ ) - 1 ) * 13 = 0
14 ! so, the firt 13 positions in the vector MAP will store the index of the neighbour of cell 1
15 ! For the cell 2
16 ! ICELL(1,1,2) = 2
17 ! IMAP = (ICELL ( IX, IY, IZ ) - 1 ) * 13 = 13
18 ! so, the positions from 14 to 26 in the vector MAP will store the index of the neighbour of cell 2
